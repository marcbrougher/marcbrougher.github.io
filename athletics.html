The Story

Goalkeeper. Captain. Scholar.

I came to UC Berkeley as a recruited Division I goalkeeper and spent four years proving that elite athletics and elite academics are not mutually exclusive. Balancing a rigorous Mechanical Engineering curriculum — often 18+ units per semester — with a full D1 training schedule required the kind of time management, mental toughness, and discipline that no classroom can teach on its own.

In my breakout season as the full-time starter, I played every minute of 17 games and ranked second at Cal in minutes played (1,503). On a statistical level, I finished second in saves (61), third in save percentage (.735), and fifth in shutouts in the entire 15-team ACC. Two moments defined that season: a career-high seven-save shutout in a road upset of then-No. 1 Pitt, and a five-save clean sheet at then-No. 2 Duke in the ACC Tournament quarterfinals.

"A star in the classroom and on the field, Brougher had a breakout season in his first full year as the starter in goal… one of his shutouts came during the regular season in Cal's 1-0 upset at then-No. 1 Pitt, with Brougher making a career-high seven saves to safeguard the victory… that earned Brougher the ACC Defensive Player of the Week award and a spot on the Top Drawer Soccer Team of the Week." — Cal Athletics

Beyond my own play, I served as team captain and as a student-athlete representative on the ACC Men's Soccer Student Athlete Advisory Committee (SAAC), advocating for student-athletes across the conference. I also co-founded the Berkeley Engineering Athlete Network, a mentorship club connecting student-athletes in engineering with industry and alumni resources.

Professional Path

Turning the Page

My performance at Cal drew professional interest. I participated in trials with MLS clubs and received professional contract offers from other organizations. After careful consideration, I made the deliberate decision to retire from competitive soccer at the conclusion of my college career.

The discipline, resilience, and competitive drive that made me a D1 goalkeeper don't disappear — they transfer. I'm channeling everything that sports built in me into engineering: the same preparation, the same high standards, and the same relentlessness that I brought to every match. The game is different. The mindset isn't.

I'm now focused entirely on building a career at the intersection of mechanical engineering, embedded systems, and intelligent design — and I'm approaching that with the same intensity I brought to every game.

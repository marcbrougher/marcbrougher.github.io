About Me

Engineer.
Athlete. Builder.

I'm a senior at UC Berkeley's College of Engineering pursuing a degree in Mechanical Engineering. For the past four years I've trained and competed as a Division I goalkeeper for the Cal Bears while maintaining a 3.686 cumulative GPA and a 3.793 technical GPA, earning Academic All-American honors along the way.

Balancing the demands of a top-tier engineering program with the relentless schedule of D1 soccer has sharpened something more valuable than any single skill: a disciplined, outcome-driven work ethic. Early mornings, tight deadlines, and high-stakes performance taught me to show up prepared, adapt fast, and never stop improving.

My love for learning runs deeper than coursework. I'm endlessly curious about how things work. That curiosity always leads me back to building things. Give me a problem, some parts, and enough time and I'll figure it out. Whether it's a PI-controlled T-shirt folder, an IoT drink dispenser, or an electric go-kart, I gravitate toward projects that live at the intersection of mechanical ingenuity and embedded software.

Outside the classroom and the training pitch, you'll find me in the mountains, on the water, or somewhere with a gradient. I grew up competing in soccer and skiing at the highest high-school levels, and that love for the outdoors never left.
